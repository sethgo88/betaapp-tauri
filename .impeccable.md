## Design Context

### Users
Seth — solo climber at the moment, with plans to open to other climbers. Used at the crag mid-session (quick logging, gloves off, sun glare) and at home after climbing (reviewing, journaling, reflecting). Dual-context means the UI must be legible at a glance and rewarding to linger in.

### Brand Personality
Clean, journaling, informative. Not sporty or aggressive. This is a personal record — more like a climbing journal that also happens to be functional software than a fitness tracker trying to gamify you.

### Aesthetic Direction
**References (positive):**
- **Claude Android** — clean, purposeful, refined. Not trying too hard, but clearly considered.
- **Drops (language app)** — bold visual hierarchy, confidence in typography, strong sense of scale contrast between elements.
- **SmartThings** — background gradients used purposefully, strong icon language, surfaces that feel layered without being glassmorphism.
- **Mountain Project** — useful information architecture (not the aesthetics).

**Anti-references:**
- **Kaya** — avoid outright (the other climbing app; generic sports app aesthetic).
- **Amazon** — too cluttered, too much competing for attention.
- **Strava** — acceptable but not inspiring; the sports-app-orange-on-dark is tired.

**Theme:** Light mode only. Warm off-white surfaces (`#faf6f1` page, `#ffffff` cards), teal primary accent (`#0d9488`), amber secondary (`#d97706`). White text on teal surfaces (`text-on-dark` token). The earth palette stays warm and distinctive; light mode is legible in sun glare without dark reflections.

### Design Principles
1. **Legibility at a glance** — any card or detail must be scannable in 2 seconds. Climbers check phones quickly mid-session.
2. **Journal, not dashboard** — data is presented to be reflected on, not to gamify. Avoid metric-hero layouts, progress bars for progress bars' sake.
3. **Bold hierarchy, calm surfaces** — big type contrast, but surfaces stay quiet. Drops-style scale, SmartThings-style depth.
4. **Touch-first and generous** — every interactive element needs real touch target size (min 44×44px). No tiny tap zones.
5. **Warm and personal** — this is *your* climbing history. The design should feel like it belongs to you, not like generic SaaS.
